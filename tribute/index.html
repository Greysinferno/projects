Randall William Rhoades

December 6th, 1956 - March 19th, 1982


The late Randy Rhoades was a phenomenal guitarist. Though his height was short lived, he lives on in the hearts of those who knew him and any guitarist that has ever played a Jackson; the signature guitar series inspired by none other than the player himself: Randy Rhoades.

As long as I'm satisfied with my work, I'm really not too concerned with what any critics think. My type of music will never be a critcal favorite, but when I can stand on stage and see a lot of smiling faces in the crowd it makes it all worthwhile. - Randy Rhoades

  • Randy was born at St. John's Hospital in Santa Monica, California on Dec 6th, 1956
  • Randy started guitar lessons around 6 or 7 years old.
  • He became interested in rock guitar at the age of 12. Leaving his piano lessons behind that his mom insisted on.
  • At the age of 14, Randy joined his first band; Violet Fox, playing rhythm guitar.
  • Around the same time Randy started playing with Quiet Riot, then know as "Little Women."
  • In late 1979 he auditioned for Ozzy Osbourne, former Black Sabbath singer.
  • Ozzy, being so impressed just by his warm-up, instantly gave him the job at the age of 22.
  • He would record 2 wonderful albums with Ozzy; Blizzard of Oz, and Diary of a Madman.
  • Randy became more and more obsessed with classical guitar as his fame grew with Ozzy.
  • March 18th, 1982, Ozzy played what would be the last show with Randy.
  • March 19th, around 9am, Randy and Andrew Aycock would fatefully crash a plane and end their lives.
  • Randy was put to rest in San Bernadion, California.
